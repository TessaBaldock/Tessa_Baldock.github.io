My Teaching Philosophy

There is so much that goes into being a Teacher. This isnt just a 9-3 job, while the school day may end for the students, for teachers it is different. We often have more work to do, activites to prepare for tomorrow's lessons, marking, assessment grading, and whatever we dont get done at school will most likely come home with us . As a teacher you give countless unpaid hours to the job, we put a lot of effort in behind the scenes, a lot of people don't realise this, but I know better then anyone. Both my mum and my grandmother were teachers, so I guess it wasnt the question of whether I will become a teacher, it was more a question of who/what I would teach.

I believe that a classroom should be a collaborative, inlcusive and fun environment where students are able to participate in classwork. Within a classroom, students not only learn content but through a collaborative classroom environment students develop social skills and friendships, they also grow as an individual. As a teacher we spend about 40 weeks of the year with our students, so we see the growth happen through the year.

I beleive that classwork and activites should be fun and engaging. They should challenge students who are at any learning level. Activites within TAS subjects should not only involve literacy and numeracy skills, but they should also foster critical and creative thinking. Within my classroom, I want students to be challenged, to be encouraged to think outside the box with the challenges that they are faced with. I believe that when students are challenged, for some only slightly, they are able to come up with original and creative ideas and potential solutions for problems posed. I do understand that not all students will be on the same learning level and as a outcome I may need to provide differentiated work to different students, this may be easier work or more challenging work. I believe that it is our responsibility, as teachers, to ensure that all students are able to make an attempt at the activities that we provide and by providing this we are allowing students an opportunity to complete the work at their level.

As a teacher, I want my classroom to be a safe environment where students feel supported to give their best attempt to all classwork, activities and assessments
